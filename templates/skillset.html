Soon after graduation, I found myself scrambling to build my resume, build a network of contacts, gain valuable insight into the hiring process, and basically be in a good position to obtain a job or start a career with a strong backing and portfolio at my disposal. Whilst perusing for better ways of managing the myriad of methods resumes are created and maintained, I came across JSON Resume.

JSON Resume is a fantastic idea. The basic premise involves collecting and formatting pieces of information commonly associated with resumes (anything from basic contact information, to education and skills) into a JSON object. This object can then be used to generate PDF copies or websites of a resume with a variety of themes, and create a standard for developers to share that information.
